# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove team impediments, and coach the team on agile best practices. They enable continuous improvement and help maintain psychological safety in iterative delivery environments.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that prevent team progress
- Coach team members on agile frameworks and best practices
- Track and encourage process improvements from retrospectives
- Shield the team from external distractions and scope creep
- Maintain sprint metrics and burndown tracking

### Goals
- Maintain team velocity and sustainable pace
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Ensure agile ceremonies are effective and time-boxed
- Reduce impediments and unplanned disruptions

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation
- Sprint planning and retrospective leadership
- One-on-ones with team members to address concerns
- Escalation of blockers to Project Manager or Product Manager

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Removes technical blockers, supports estimation accuracy
- **Project Manager**: Escalates risks and dependencies, provides team capacity insights
- **Product Manager**: Ensures backlog clarity supports sprint execution

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers conduct user research, create prototypes and wireframes, and validate usability to ensure features meet customer needs and deliver excellent user experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Validate design concepts with users and stakeholders
- Deliver design systems, asset libraries, and style guides
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Iterate on designs based on feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Ensure product usability and user satisfaction
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Create consistent and accessible design experiences
- Minimize rework due to design-development misalignment

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with stakeholders
- Design specifications and component documentation
- Feedback loops with developers during implementation
- User testing reports and insights sharing

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Aligns design direction with product vision and user research
- **Developers**: Partners on implementation, reviews code against design specs
- **Project Manager**: Provides design timelines and dependency information

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts clarify requirements, translate business needs into actionable specifications, and analyze processes to identify optimization opportunities. They bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create detailed specifications and user stories with acceptance criteria
- Support requirements validation and user acceptance testing
- Identify process dependencies and integration points
- Facilitate communication between business and technical teams

### Goals
- Ensure delivered solutions solve the right business problem
- Reduce rework due to unclear or misunderstood requirements
- Improve process efficiency and stakeholder satisfaction
- Enable data-driven decision-making through analysis

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Detailed specification documents and process diagrams
- User acceptance testing and validation sessions
- Root cause analysis for post-implementation issues

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Refines product requirements and success metrics
- **Project Manager**: Provides scope clarity and dependency information
- **Developers**: Answers clarifying questions during implementation
- **QA/Testing**: Supports test plan development and UAT execution

---

## System Architect

### Role Summary
System Architects design technical architecture, ensure scalability and maintainability, and align system design with business requirements. They provide technical leadership on major design decisions and trade-offs.

### Responsibilities
- Design system architecture aligned with business and technical requirements
- Evaluate technology choices and architectural patterns
- Ensure scalability, reliability, and security of systems
- Document architectural decisions and trade-offs
- Advise on technical feasibility and risk mitigation
- Review major design changes for architectural alignment
- Mentor developers on architectural principles

### Goals
- Build scalable, maintainable, and resilient systems
- Reduce technical debt and rework
- Enable teams to make consistent, well-reasoned technical decisions
- Minimize architectural issues discovered late in development

### Typical Communication
- Architecture design documents and decision logs
- Technical design review sessions
- Consultations on major feature implementations
- Technology evaluation and recommendation reports

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Advises on technical timelines and dependencies
- **Developers**: Partners on detailed design and implementation strategy
- **Scrum Master**: Identifies technical impediments and improvement areas
- **Product Manager**: Aligns technical approach with product roadmap
